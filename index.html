ABOUT THE ROHINGYA REFUGEE CRISIS

In August 2017, a major humanitarian crisis unfolded when about 700,000 Rohingya people fled violence in Myanmar, seeking refuge in Bangladesh. They joined earlier waves of refugees, leading to one of the world's largest displacement situations. The Bangladeshi government and local communities opened their borders, offering critical shelter in places like Cox's Bazar and Bhasan Char. This generosity, with support from international aid, has provided vital relief, but many refugees still face a future of uncertainty and limited access to resources.
